Exercise 3 - Sorting Customer Orders
=====================================

Objective:
Sort customer orders by total price to prioritize high-value orders for processing.

Sorting Algorithms Overview:
- Bubble Sort:    Repeatedly swaps adjacent elements if they are out of order
- Insertion Sort: Builds a sorted portion one element at a time from left to right
- Quick Sort:     Picks a pivot, partitions the array, and recursively sorts each half
- Merge Sort:     Splits the array in half, sorts each half, then merges them back

Bubble Sort Time Complexity:
- Best:    O(n) if already sorted (with optimization)
- Average: O(n^2)
- Worst:   O(n^2)

Quick Sort Time Complexity:
- Best:    O(n log n)
- Average: O(n log n)
- Worst:   O(n^2) - rare, occurs with bad pivot selection on already sorted data

Why Quick Sort is preferred:
Quick Sort is significantly faster in practice due to better cache performance and
lower constant factors. For 1000 orders, Bubble Sort could take ~1,000,000 operations
while Quick Sort typically needs around 10,000. For real e-commerce platforms handling
thousands of daily orders, Quick Sort is the practical and scalable choice.
