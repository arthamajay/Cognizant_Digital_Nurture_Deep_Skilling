Exercise 1 - Inventory Management System
=========================================

Objective:
Build an inventory system that stores, updates, and removes products efficiently.

Solution:
A HashMap is used to store products with the productId as the key. This gives direct
access to any product without looping through the entire list.

Data Structure Choice - HashMap:
- Add:    O(1) average - insert by key directly
- Update: O(1) average - look up by key, then modify
- Delete: O(1) average - remove by key directly

Why HashMap over ArrayList?
An ArrayList would require O(n) to find a product by ID. With HashMap, the key-based
lookup makes all three operations constant time on average, which is essential when
the inventory grows to thousands of products.

Optimization:
Using productId as the key means no linear scanning is ever needed. For range-based
queries (e.g., all products under $50), a TreeMap could be considered for sorted access.
